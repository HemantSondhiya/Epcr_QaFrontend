# 🚑 Paramedic Guide: How It Works & Your Responsibilities

Welcome to the Paramedic guide! This document explains how you use the application to save lives and document patient care. It is designed to be simple, clear, and easy to follow.

---

## ❓ Are You Responsible for Creating ePCRs and Updating History?

**Yes, absolutely!** 
*   **Creating ePCRs**: This is your primary responsibility in the app. Every time you respond to a call, you will create a new Electronic Patient Care Report (ePCR).
*   **Updating Patient History**: When you fill out the patient's current condition and past history in a new ePCR, you are actively updating the system's knowledge of that patient. While you may not edit past records, your new record becomes the latest chapter in that patient's medical history.

---

## 🛠️ Your Toolbox (What You Can Access)

When you log in, you will see a clean dashboard with access to:
*   **📊 Dashboard**: A quick summary of your recent work and status.
*   **📝 EPCR**: Your main workspace to create new patient records.
*   **💬 Feedback**: Messages from QA Reviewers if they need you to clarify something.
*   **🔔 Notifications**: Alerts about record approvals or requested changes.
*   **📂 Patient History**: Look up past records to see a patient's medical background before you even arrive!

---

## 🚶‍♂️ A Day in the Life: The Paramedic Flow

Here is the specific flow of how you work with the application during a call:

### 1. Fill the Current ePCR 📝
When you respond to a call, your first step is to fill out the current ePCR.
*   **Action**: Open the app and start a **New ePCR**.
*   **Crucial Step**: Make sure to enter the patient's **Phone Number** in the Patient Information section. This phone number will be used to link everything together!

### 2. Search in Patient History 📂
If you need to view or update the patient's long-term history:
*   **Action**: Go to the **Patient History** portal in the app.
*   **Search**: Type the **Phone Number** you just created/entered in the ePCR into the search bar.
*   **Select**: Click on the patient's profile to open their history.

### 3. Review and Update the Portal 🔄
In the Patient History portal, you will see a detailed dashboard with the following sections:
*   **📋 Overview**: A quick summary of the patient's profile and critical alerts.
*   **💊 Medications**: A list of current and past prescribed medicines.
*   **🤝 Encounters**: History of past emergency calls and visits.
*   **🏥 Admissions**: Records of hospital admissions.
*   **🧪 Labs**: Laboratory test results and findings.
*   **💓 Vitals**: Historical data charts for heart rate, BP, etc.
*   **📄 Documents**: Access to previous ePCRs and clinical documents.
*   **⏱️ Timeline**: A chronological view of the patient's medical events.

You can use this portal to review the patient's background and update their history based on your current encounter!

## 🧠 Detailed Guide: Filling Past & Current History in the ePCR

Capturing a patient's history is like putting together a puzzle. Here is how to do it quickly and accurately in **Step 1** of the ePCR form:

### 1. How to get the info:
*   **Ask the Patient**: *"Do you have any medical conditions or allergies?"*
*   **Ask Family**: If the patient can't talk, ask family members or bystanders.
*   **Look for Clues**: Check for medical bracelets or medication bottles on scene.

### 2. What to fill in the App:
*   **Comorbidity**: Type in existing conditions (e.g., "Diabetes", "Asthma").
*   **Current Medicines**: List what they are currently taking (e.g., "Lisinopril").
*   **Allergy**: Type any allergies. If they have none, simply type **NKA** (No Known Allergies).
*   **Toggles**: Use the quick switches for **Smoker**, **Alcohol Use**, and **DNR** (Do Not Resuscitate).

---

## 🏁 What Happens After You Click Save?

1.  **QA Review**: Your record goes to a Quality Assurance reviewer.
2.  **Great Job!**: If everything is correct, they approve it, and it becomes a permanent part of the patient's history.
3.  **Need Fixes?**: If they need more info, you will get a **Notification**. You can open the record, make the edits, and save it again.

This system ensures that patients get the best care and that their medical records are always accurate!
