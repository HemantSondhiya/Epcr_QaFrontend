# 👨‍⚕️ Physician & Doctor Guide: How It Works & Your Flow

Welcome to the Physician and Doctor guide! This document explains how you use the application for clinical oversight, reviewing records, and managing patient history.

> **Note**: In this system, both "Doctors" and "Physicians" use the **PHYSICIAN** role.

---

## 📋 Role Overview
As a **Physician**, your role is focused on **oversight, review, and high-level clinical decision-making**. You typically don't create the initial ePCR (the Paramedics do that), but you review them, check QA outcomes, and analyze patient data to ensure quality care.

---

## 🛠️ Your Toolbox (What You Can Access)

When you log in, you will see a professional dashboard with access to:
*   **📊 Dashboard**: Overview of system activity and critical alerts.
*   **📝 EPCR**: View electronic Patient Care Reports created by paramedics.
*   **⚖️ QA Reviews**: Review the quality scores and feedback on records.
*   **📈 Reports**: Access high-level clinical and operational analytics.
*   **💬 Feedback**: Provide or read feedback on specific records.
*   **🔔 Notifications**: Alerts about records needing your attention.
*   **📂 Patient History**: View the complete, longitudinal medical history of any patient.

---

## 🚶‍♂️ The Physician Workflow

Here is how you typically interact with the application:

### 1. Reviewing Complex Cases (EPCR & QA Reviews) 🔍
*   **Action**: You receive notifications about critical or complex calls (e.g., severe trauma or cardiac arrest).
*   **Flow**: You navigate to the **EPCR** or **QA Reviews** section to review the record filled out by the paramedic. You check if protocols were followed and if the treatment was appropriate.

### 2. Providing Clinical Feedback 💬
*   **Action**: Based on your review, you can leave feedback.
*   **Flow**: In the **Feedback** section, you can write notes for the paramedic or the QA team to improve care quality or correct documentation.

### 3. Analyzing Patient History 📂
*   **Action**: For patients under your care or for follow-up review.
*   **Flow**: 
    1. Go to the **Patient History** portal.
    2. Search for the patient (by name, phone, or email).
    3. Review their comprehensive history, including **Overview, Medications, Encounters, Admissions, Labs, Vitals, Documents, and Timeline**.
    4. This helps you understand long-term trends or the impact of emergency interventions.

### 4. Viewing Reports and Analytics 📈
*   **Action**: To understand organization-wide clinical performance.
*   **Flow**: You access the **Reports** section to see statistics on response times, treatment success rates, and common clinical presentations.

---

## 🏁 Summary of Differences (Paramedic vs. Physician)

*   **Paramedic**: **Creates** the data on scene (fills ePCR, takes vitals, logs medications).
*   **Physician**: **Reviews** and **Analyzes** the data (oversees QA, reviews history, checks reports).

This separation ensures that paramedics can focus on fast, effective care while you ensure the highest standards of medical quality!
